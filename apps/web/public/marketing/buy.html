The Process

Four steady steps from search to closing.

1. Align goals

Clarify budget, timing, and neighborhoods; set must-haves and deal-breakers up front.

2. Tour with clarity

Walk each home with a clear checklist and decide when to bring in licensed inspectors or engineers for expert findings.

3. Offer with clarity

Use comps, disclosures, and inspection findings to shape offer terms and inspection responses.

4. Close and plan ahead

Stay organized through appraisal and closing with a written plan for any post-close priorities.

Why buyers hire a REALTOR® + builder

Most tours focus on finishes. We focus on decision quality: market context, property disclosures, and a practical due-diligence plan. A builder license supports scope and risk conversations, while licensed inspectors and engineers provide formal findings when appropriate. If work is needed, we help you get independent bids from licensed contractors you choose.

What we review with you before you offer

  • Questions to ask about structure, roof, drainage, and visible wear
  • Which systems to verify through licensed inspections
  • Where specialist input (engineer/trade) may be appropriate
  • How written bids can inform credits, price, or scope decisions

Buyer FAQs

Do you push unnecessary work?

No. We suggest scope only when it supports your due-diligence plan and offer strategy.

Can you help me plan costs before I write?

Yes. We help you identify likely scope and, when needed, gather written bids from licensed contractors you choose.

Will you attend the inspection?

Yes. We attend and help you review findings, and we encourage licensed inspectors and engineers whenever specialist input is appropriate.

Can you help if work is needed?

Yes. If work is needed, we help you get independent bids from licensed contractors you choose.

Can you help after closing?

Yes. We can help you prioritize next steps and compare written bids from licensed contractors you choose.
