Why buyers hire a REALTOR® + builder

Most tours focus on finishes. We focus on the parts that drive safety, cost, and value. With a builder-trained eye beside you, you see the true condition of each home, understand what matters and what doesn’t, and get estimated ranges for repairs and improvements before you commit. Your offers are grounded in data and clear scope—not guesswork. You stay in control, move at the right speed for you, and land a home that fits both your budget and your standards in West Michigan.

What we evaluate before you offer

  • Structure: foundation, roof, framing, load paths, movement
  • Systems: electrical, plumbing, HVAC age and serviceability
  • Moisture: grading, drainage, gutters, signs of intrusion
  • Envelope: windows, siding, flashing, insulation basics
  • Interior condition: subfloor, drywall, trim, cabinetry fit
  • Safety: egress, GFCI, rails, detectors, visible code items
  • Mechanical lifespan: likely replacements and timing
  • Cost to remedy: labor, materials, permits, timelines
  • Cost to improve: value-add scenarios and phasing
  • Appraisal context: comps plus improvement impact
  • Neighborhood fit: schools, commute, resale considerations
  • Trade readiness: who to involve and when

Buyer FAQs

Do you push renovations?

No. We only suggest work when it meaningfully protects or improves value.

Can you estimate ranges before I write?

Yes. We provide estimated repair and improvement ranges before you bid.

Will you attend the inspection?

Yes. We review findings with a builder-trained lens and help prioritize responses.

Do you coordinate trades?

Yes. We bring vetted partners in when needed and manage to a clear scope.

Can you help after closing?

Yes. We can plan and oversee post-close punch lists or light improvements.
