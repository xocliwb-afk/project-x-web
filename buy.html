How We Help Buyers in Grand Rapids

Step 1: Strategy & Search

Define move-in ready, light refresh, or project home. We filter by location, budget, and true condition to save time and avoid dead ends.

Step 2: On-Site Evaluation & Diligence

We review roof, foundation, HVAC, electrical, plumbing, moisture, windows, insulation, and life-cycle items. You get clear ranges for repair or renovation where relevant.

Step 3: Offer & Negotiation

We justify price with comps and documented costs, negotiate credits or repairs when appropriate, and keep the deal aligned to your priorities.

Step 4: Closing & After Close

No pressure to renovate. If you want updates, we provide a right-sized plan with timelines and estimates. If not, you move in with confidence.

What We Evaluate Before You Write

  • Structural movement, water management, and drainage
  • Roof age, flashing, ventilation, and attic health
  • Electrical panel capacity, bonding, and visible safety issues
  • HVAC age, performance, and expected remaining life
  • Plumbing supply, drains, and leak risk areas
  • Basement and crawlspace moisture indicators
  • Windows, insulation, and energy-efficiency upgrades
  • Code/safety items that can derail an appraisal
  • Cosmetic vs. structural work to avoid overspending

Buyer FAQs

Do you recommend renovating every home?

No. We only suggest work when it improves net value or reduces risk. Many clients buy move-in ready. The builder advantage is optional.

Can you estimate renovation costs before I write an offer?

Yes. We provide realistic ranges common to Grand Rapids real estate so you can price with confidence.

Will you attend the inspection?

Yes. We review findings through a licensed builder lens and help prioritize what matters for safety, value, and negotiation.

How do you get paid?

We’re your licensed REALTOR®. Our compensation is typically paid through the transaction. Any post-close work is separate and always optional.
